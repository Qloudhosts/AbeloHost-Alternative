## What is DMCA Ignored Hosting?

DMCA stands for the Digital Millennium Copyright Act – a US law that allows copyright holders to file takedown requests against websites they believe are using their protected content.

When a regular hosting provider receives a DMCA complaint, they're typically required to suspend the accused website, often without verifying whether the claim is legitimate. This can result in your site going offline over a false or frivolous claim.

**DMCA Ignored Hosting** means the hosting provider operates from a country outside US jurisdiction (like the Netherlands, Moldova, Iceland, or Malaysia) and is not legally bound to comply with DMCA takedown requests. Your content stays online as long as it's legal under the host country's laws.

This type of hosting is essential for website owners running adult content platforms, streaming sites, casino and gambling websites, torrent-related sites, forums with user-generated content, political or controversial blogs, and any site that might face frivolous copyright claims.

> ⚠️ **Important:** DMCA ignored doesn't mean lawless. These hosting providers still follow local laws. Your content must be legal under the jurisdiction where the server is located. DMCA-ignored hosting simply protects you from US-based copyright takedown mechanisms.
